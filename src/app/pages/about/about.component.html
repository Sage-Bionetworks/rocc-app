The ROCC Vision

The Challenge experience is currently cumbersome for all stakeholders involved. Participants encounter a huge pain-point in discovering Challenges they are interested in, while Organizers struggle to find a streamlined solution to reaching a wide audience.

The Registry of Community Challenges (ROCC) strives to address some of the friction in the Challenge experience by providing a centralized hub to reduce participant search cost and increasing Challenge visibility. Think of the ROCC like an EventBrite for the scientific benchmarking space!

Background

Sage Bionetworks strives to bridge the translational divide between biomedical research and clinical application by embracing open and reusable practices. One example is hosting scientific Challenges, galvanizing large communities of experts around new and complex biomedical problems. Aside from crowdsourcing biomedical problems, Challenges also seek to enable the robust assessment of tool performance (Ellrott, K., Buchanan, A., Creason, A. et al.)

When done right, Challenges encourage innovation by promoting friendly competition to develop the best algorithms, evaluation with objective frameworks for assessment of these algorithms, and collaboration by bringing together new teams to uncover new insights. The Challenges ecosystem, however, is bottlenecked by the lack of a hub centralizing all biomedical challenges. Participants encounter a huge pain-point in discovering Challenges they are interested in, while Organizers struggle to find a streamlined solution to reaching a wide audience.

The Problem

A successful Challenge requires both organizers and participants. While existing Challenge platforms like Synapse, Kaggle and EvalAI help connect organizers and participants and run challenges, the visibility of challenges is usually limited to the users of a specific challenge platform. The multiplication of challenge platforms and organizations leads to a lack of a central challenge hub that connects more widely users at the level of the entire biomedical community. We summarize our target users as follows:

  • Challenge Participants are typically researchers or engineers. They want to solve challenging biomedical problems while building their professional reputation, and discover conference or Challenge opportunities to support both those aspirations.
  • Challenge Organizers are often Principal Investigators and researchers. They want to host a successful Challenge event, where a diverse network of researchers come together and spur innovation, without too much event overhead.

Challenge participants face the headache of finding relevant challenges, while Challenge organizers struggle with reaching the most wide and relevant audience. Here are the three biggest pain-points we hear from both sides:

  • Fragmented information ecosystem: over 25 organizations run biomedical challenges, each with their own website and social media channels (e.g: DREAM Challenges, CAFA, CAGI, MICCAI, TREC).
  • Lack of standardizations: each of those 25+ organizations lays out Challenge information differently, requiring organizers to repeatedly enter details on each site and introducing a poor search experience for participants (e.g: searching for “carcinoma” instead of “cancer” and getting zero results).
  • High overhead with Challenge promotion: Organizers face high cognitive overload due to the need to identify the owners of multiple communication channels (e.g. mailing lists and social media accounts) currently required to widely connect with the biomedical community.

The ROCC aims to tackle each of these problems.

Goals

  1. Make Challenges more discoverable.
    • Standardize information about Challenges
    • Create a community hub
  2. Empower all Challenge ecosystem participants to manage their professional identity
    • Help organizers drive thought, leadership, and awareness
    • Support participants with their professional development journeys
  3. Empower organizers with Challenge intelligence
    • Create ROCC user profiles to enable Challenge intelligence
    • Require registered ROCC users to go through an onboarding fllow where they populate the Challenge they have been a part of
  4. Promote discoverability and reusability of Challenge artifacts
